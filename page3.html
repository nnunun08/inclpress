Paper weight refers to how thick or heavy the paper is, and it's usually measured in gsm, pounds (lb), or points (pt).

• gsm (grams per square meter) is the most common and accurate unit, used globally.
• lb (pound) is mainly used in the U.S. for thinner papers like inside pages (text stock).
• pt (point) is used for thicker papers like covers, where 1 pt = 0.001 inches.

There are two main types of paper stock:
• Text stock (also called oﬀset paper) is thinner and used for inner pages. It's measured in lb.
• Cover stock is thicker and sturdier, used for covers, and is measured in pt or high gsm.

It’s important to note that a higher number doesn’t always mean thicker paper.
For example, 100 lb text is actually thinner than 65 lb cover because they use diﬀerent base sizes.

As a general reference:
10 pt ≈ 250 gsm, 12 pt ≈ 300 gsm, 14 pt ≈ 350 gsm.

Proofing

E Proof PDF Proo

E Proof is a fast and convenient way to check your files before printing. It allows you to review layout, text, and general design, but its not suitable for checking color accuracy or image quality. This method is recommended for most standard orders.

Hard Copy Proof

Hard copy proof is a printed and bound sample of your project, usually ready to ship within 1 2 days. Its ideal for checking colors and image quality, but may differ slightly from the final offset print in paper or finish. Special features like foil or embossing are not included, and hardcover or wire coil books are proofed in perfect binding format.

Shrink Wrapping (Optional)

Shrink wrapping option per copy or multiple copies. The minimum and maximum quantity per bundle varies by size and thickness of the online printing product ordered. When you place an order, write the number of copies for wrapping in the additional request.

Drilling (Optional)

Drilling is available as an option for hole punching. If youd like to include it, simply select the drilling option when placing your order. If you have specific requirements such as the number or size of holes, please include them in the Additional Requests section during checkout.
